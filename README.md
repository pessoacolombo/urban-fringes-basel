## About this guide

This document is the fieldwork guide for **Brief 3: Building as Catalyst**, part of the course *Urbanisms of the Health Industry* (UAG Track, MA Critical Urbanism, University of Basel).

The exercise asks students to systematically observe and record life in the **fringe spaces** between the Roche and Novartis pharmaceutical campuses and the surrounding urban fabric of Basel — following Jan Gehl's eye-level approach and Public Space / Public Life (PSPL) survey methodology.

## Three-pillar methodology

The exercise is structured around three complementary forms of fieldwork:

1. **Spatial Description** — map the fringe zone at 1:200, recording land cover, façade materiality, and physical barriers
2. **Structured Observation** — count and classify users at fixed spots across four 15-minute windows (09:00 / 12:00 / 15:00 / 17:00), following Gehl's activity categories
3. **Short Survey** — structured questionnaire (frequency, usage, profile) with a minimum of 12 participants per fringe area

## Course context

> *"The campuses of Roche and Novartis are not merely work places or architectural landmarks — they become urban actors. Each campus occupies a substantial share of Basel's built fabric, enclosed by fences, gates, security checkpoints, and the quiet signals of institutional territory. But what happens at the boundary between these campuses and the surrounding city?"*

The exercise feeds into Brief 3's broader inquiry into campus urbanism, enclosure, and the relationship between pharmaceutical institutions and public life in Basel. Findings will be presented at the final exhibition at the **Atrium of the Department of Construction and Transport, Münsterplatz 11, Basel**.
